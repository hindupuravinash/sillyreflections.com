House Sparrow

  • Birds / Urban Birding / Wildlife
  • 26 Jan, 2013
  • 1

House Sparrows were perhaps the most numerous birds in India till recent times, but now they are reduced to a state where many of current generation don’t even know or saw a Sparrow in reality! These small passerines are always seen in pairs and flock together while foraging for food, be it grains or small insects and are highly vocal. The chirrups of Sparrows are still common in rural parts of India.

House Sparrows have attracted a lot of national and international attention due to their sharp decrease in numbers, but I am glad to notice that Kolkata, being a metropolis, is still home for a large number of these tiny birds!  For other birds spotted in campus, have a look at this checklist and photos!

 

Common Name: House Sparrow                                              Scientific Name: Passer domesticus

Local Names: Assamese:                                                             Size: 12-15 cms

Global Status: Least Threatened

Distinctive Identifiers:

1.Grey Crown in males

2.Black throat and upper breast

3.Chestnut colored body in males, dull brown in females

4. Grayish white underparts

5. White ear-coverts on male whereas brown faint supercilium on females
