I cannot say it in one sentence other than to say that I love it and I am at peace when I go birding! Perhaps next time, I will come up with some points and describe how it feels when I’m out there so that you can decide if you wanna begin bird watching!

Here is a picture taken right in the middle of a metro city – Chennai! It is a very popular birding place called Pallikkaranai Marsh, often said to be “Green Lungs of the City”, it faces the same threats as other wetlands all over the world – human encroachment, drying up, pollution. But when I stand there watching the birds, I forget the surroundings, the blaring horns, the road-side dust or burning sun, I just keep watching!!

Common Pigeons are perhaps the most seen species in urban spaces, and for many children in them, birds means pigeons! The feral populations of Common Pigeon, also called as Rock Dove, have adapted themselves very much to survive in cities and human settlements. The grey birds with iridescence on their necks are found in groups and pairs foraging for food or roosting on high structures of the vicinity.

Meaning of Scientific Name:

The Common Birds are called as Columba livia from Latin which was in turn derived from Greek. Columba is from kolumbos, a diver while livia is obtained from peleia ie. Greek for dove, supposedly derived from pellos dark colored referring to the coloring of these birds!

Common Name: Common Pigeon                                             Scientific Name: Columba livia

Local Names: Telugu:                                                                       Size: 30-35  cms

Global Status: Least Threatened

Distinctive Identifiers:

1. Grey tail and body

2. Two bands of black on wings

3. Red legs

4. Red eyes

5. Iridescence in neck

]]> https://sillyreflections.com/2013/02/14/common-pigeon/feed/ 0 Tickell’s Thrushhttps://sillyreflections.com/2013/02/13/tickells-thrush/ https://sillyreflections.com/2013/02/13/tickells-thrush/#comments Wed, 13 Feb 2013 18:21:13 +0000 https://sillyreflections.com/?p=1447 Tickell’s Thrush (Turdus unicolor)

Tickell’s Thrushes are a species in the family of Thrushes and are among those birds on which one cannot gather much information on the internet. The only confusable species with Tickell’s Thrush is the Indian Blackbird, whose females can be quite similar to 1st winter males or females of Tickell’s Thrush. The major distinctive feature among these is the vivid dark stripe on the females, while the males are slight pale blue shade of grey in color. One can also observe a dark olive color wash on upperparts.

Tickell’s Thrush, while belonging to Thrush family which are songbirds, have very less vocal activity. Generally one can find Tickell’s Thrush deep inside canopy trees in big gardens or plantations during winter.

Meaning of Scientific Name:

Tickell’s Thrush is called Turdus unicolor which is Latin for “thrush of uniform color” which describes its appearance colorwise and attributes by mentioning that its a songbird(thrush).

Common Name: Tickell’s Thrush                                            Scientific Name: Turdus unicolor

Local Names: Assamese:                                                                       Size: 18-22 cms

Global Status: Least Threatened

Distinctive Identifiers:

1. White belly graduated from breast

2. Yellow beak

3. Yellow eye ring

4. Dark stripe on sides of white throat on females

5. Spottings on throat extending from the stripe on females and juveniles.
